The best moments on *Etched in Sound* arrive as quiet rearrangements rather than sudden hooks. Amber Vale builds the album like a room you keep returning to, rearranging the furniture each time you listen.

The low end is soft, almost architectural, and the vocal production is patient and unforced. It is not a record for skipping around. Start at track one, let it run, and it turns into a long-form mood that feels lived in rather than performed.

What makes this one stick is restraint. The best tracks avoid the obvious climax and instead trade on detail: background synths that pulse like a dim hallway light, hand percussion that feels human, and a closing track that lands like a sigh.
